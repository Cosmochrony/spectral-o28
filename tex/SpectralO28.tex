  \begin{abstract}
    The O25 programme~\cite{Beau2026a25} identified the ratio $n_1(q)/q$ as the central open
    asymptotic variable controlling the finite-size drift of $\bar{\delta}_{\mathrm{pair}}(q)$ and the
    effectiveness of the O14 normalisation correction~\cite{Beau2026a14}.
    The present paper reports two measurements on the O25 checkpoints for
    $q \in \{29, 61, 101, 151, 211\}$.
    First, we extract $n_1(q)$ directly from the auto-calibrated fitting windows and fit
    $n_1(q) = \hat{\alpha}\, q + \hat{\beta}$ by ordinary least squares, obtaining
    $\hat{\alpha} \approx 0.053$ with $R^2 \approx 0.879$ and $p \approx 0.018$.
    The correction factor $\eta \log q / \log n_1(q)$ remains $O(1)$ across all tested primes,
    and the corrected exponent $\delta_{\mathrm{corr}}(q) \in [7.4, 10.6]$ for all
    $q \in \{29, 61, 101, 151, 211\}$, consistent with the admissible window
    established in O9--O14~\cite{Beau2026a9,Beau2026a14}.
    Second, we develop an inter-pair trajectory diversity proxy for the effective-dimension
    test of O26~\cite{Beau2026a26}, using the per-pair mean trajectories $\bar{\sigma}_c(n)$ stored in
    the O25 checkpoints.
    The proxy measures the number of independent decay-shape directions across pairs after
    removing the common power-law component.
    The second singular value ratio $\sigma_2 / \sigma_1$ grows monotonically with $q$
    ($0.042$ at $q = 29$ to $0.238$ at $q = 211$), providing evidence of increasing
    inter-pair trajectory diversity.
    At $q = 151$, the first prime for which the proxy is informative
    ($n_{\mathrm{win}} \geq 10$ and per-conjugate sigma vectors available), the
    participation-ratio rank is $r_{\mathrm{eff}}^{\mathrm{PR}} \approx 1.09$ and the
    $10\%$-threshold rank is $r_{\mathrm{eff}}^{10\%} = 2$; the spin-$\tfrac{1}{2}$ prediction
    $d_\rho^2 = 4$ of O26 Criterion~5.4 is not yet reached at the proxy level.
    The formal Test~4 of O26 requires per-block Weil vector trajectories not stored in
    the current pipeline and is deferred to a dedicated extension.
  \end{abstract}

  \section{Position of the Problem}

    \subsection{What O25--O27 established}

      Paper O25~\cite{Beau2026a25} established three results for the pair observable
      $\sigma_{\mathrm{pair}}(n) = \sigma_c(n)\,\sigma_{q-c}(n)$ on $\mathrm{Heis}_3(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$:
      (i) the inter-pair standard deviation of $\delta_{\mathrm{pair}}$ decreases monotonically from
      $0.54$ at $q = 29$ to $0.11$ at $q = 211$, confirming that $\delta_{\mathrm{pair}}$ is a
      structural invariant of the Weil representation;
      (ii) empirical fits of the form $\delta_\infty + a/\!\log q$ describe the data well but carry
      no asymptotic meaning, because the leading finite-size correction $\eta \log q / \log n_1(q) \approx 1$
      is itself $q$-dependent;
      (iii) when the O14 correction is applied, the corrected values $\delta_{\mathrm{corr}}(q) \in [7.7, 9.0]$
      fall within the admissible window $[7.4, 10.6]$ for $q \in \{61, 101, 151, 211\}$.
      O25 identified the ratio $n_1(q)/q$ as the central open direction.

      Paper O26~\cite{Beau2026a26} established a dictionary between conjugate Weil blocks and
      rank-one matrix coefficients in a representation space, and formulated a hierarchy of
      identifications (Levels~I--III) for the pair observable.
      Level~I (proved) establishes that $\sigma_{\mathrm{pair}}(n)$ and the Hilbert--Schmidt norm of an
      associated matrix trajectory share the same growth exponent.
      Test~4 of O26 (Criterion~5.4) provides the key falsifiability criterion: the effective
      dimension $r_{\mathrm{eff}}$ of the per-pair covariance operator
      $\mathcal{C}_c = N^{-1} \sum_n \operatorname{vec}(\widetilde{M}^f_n)\operatorname{vec}(\widetilde{M}^f_n)^\dagger$
      in $\operatorname{End}(V_\rho)$ should equal $d_\rho^2 = 4$ for the spin-$\tfrac{1}{2}$ sector candidate.

      Paper O27~\cite{Beau2026a27} resolved the existence of the admissible morphism $\Phi_{q,\rho}$
      unconditionally, proving that any morphism satisfying the three structural constraints forces
      a unique factorisation through $\mathfrak{su}(2)$.
      The full implication chain
      \[
        \text{emergence} \Rightarrow \text{non-injectivity}~\cite{Beau2026b}
        \Rightarrow \text{pair structure} \Rightarrow \text{quadratic form}
        \Rightarrow \mathfrak{su}(2)
      \]
      is thereby closed at the structural level.
      The remaining open question after O27 is the numerical identification of the correct
      irreducible sector from O25 data.

    \subsection{Scope of O28}

      O28 addresses the two numerical questions left open after O25 and O27:
      \begin{enumerate}
        \item \textbf{Part~A}: calibrate the asymptotic constant $\hat{\alpha}$ in
        $n_1(q) \sim \hat{\alpha}\, q$ and reassess $\delta_{\mathrm{corr}}(q)$ using the measured
        $n_1$ values rather than the qualitative $\Theta(q)$ scaling of O9~\cite{Beau2026a9}.
        \item \textbf{Part~B}: develop a proxy for the inter-pair trajectory diversity relevant to
        O26 Test~4, using the per-pair mean trajectories available in the O25 checkpoints, and
        identify what additional pipeline data would be required for the formal test.
      \end{enumerate}

  \section{Setup and Notation}

    We follow the notation of O25~\cite{Beau2026a25}.
    Let $q$ be an odd prime, $G_q = \mathrm{Heis}_3(\mathbb{Z}/q\mathbb{Z})$, and
    $\mathrm{Cay}(G_q, S_q)$ its Cayley graph with generating set $S_q = \{X^{\pm 1}, Y^{\pm 1}\}$.
    The BFS shells $S_n$ and ball $B_n$ are defined as in O9--O12~\cite{Beau2026a9,Beau2026a12}.
    The pair observable is
    \[
      \sigma_c(n) = \frac{\delta r_n}{|S_n|}, \qquad
      \sigma_{\mathrm{pair}}(n) = \sigma_c(n)\,\sigma_{q-c}(n),
    \]
    where $\delta r_n$ is the rank increment contributed by Weil fingerprint vectors in shell $S_n$,
    as defined in O12~\cite{Beau2026a12}.
    The fitting window $[n_0, n_1]$ is auto-calibrated from the BFS data via the
    \texttt{find\_fitting\_window} procedure of O12, with $\mathrm{bfs\_frac} = 0.99$.
    The O14 normalisation correction~\cite{Beau2026a14} gives
    \begin{equation}
      \delta_{\mathrm{corr}}(q) = \bar{\delta}_{\mathrm{pair}}(q) - \eta\,
      \frac{\log q}{\log n_1(q)}, \qquad \eta = \tfrac{1}{2}.
      \label{eq:dcorr}
    \end{equation}

    \subsection{OLS fit and asymptotic constant}

      We fit $n_1(q) = \hat{\alpha}\, q + \hat{\beta}$ by ordinary least squares over
      $q \in \{29, 61, 101, 151, 211\}$.

      \begin{proposition}[Empirical window-depth fit]
        \label{prop:ols}
        Over the range $q \in \{29, 61, 101, 151, 211\}$, the OLS fit yields
        $\hat{\alpha} \approx 0.053$ (95\% CI: $[0.017, 0.088]$),
        $\hat{\beta} \approx 4.175$, $R^2 \approx 0.879$, $p \approx 0.018$.
        The ratio $n_1(q)/q$ decreases from $0.138$ at $q = 29$ to $0.066$ at $q = 211$.
        The Window-Depth Theorem (O9, Theorem~6.1~\cite{Beau2026a9}) guarantees
        $n_1(q) = \Theta(q)$; the present fit quantifies the proportionality constant empirically
        as a lower bound.
      \end{proposition}

      \begin{remark}
        \label{rem:scenario}
        The ratio $n_1(q)/q$ is monotonically decreasing over the entire tested range, from
        $0.138$ at $q = 29$ to $0.066$ at $q = 211$.
        The asymptotic regime $n_1(q) \sim \alpha q$ has therefore not yet been reached at $q = 211$.
        Proposition~\ref{prop:ols} provides a lower-bound estimate of the asymptotic proportionality
        constant rather than a convergent identification.
        Extending the campaign to $q \in \{307, 401\}$ would constrain $\hat{\alpha}$ more tightly.
      \end{remark}

    \subsection{Stability of $\delta_{\mathrm{corr}}(q)$}

      From Table~\ref{tab:window}, the correction factor $\eta \log q / \log n_1(q)$ lies in
      $[0.96, 1.21]$ over the tested range, close to its limiting value $1$ predicted from
      $n_1(q) = \Theta(q)$.
      For all $q \in \{29, 61, 101, 151, 211\}$, the corrected exponent $\delta_{\mathrm{corr}}(q)$
      falls within the admissible window $[7.4, 10.6]$ established by O9--O14.

      This confirms the main structural conclusion of O25~\cite{Beau2026a25}: the apparent decrease
      of $\bar{\delta}_{\mathrm{pair}}(q)$ is a finite-size normalisation effect, not a structural
      deviation below the admissible window.
      The result now extends to $q = 211$, with $\delta_{\mathrm{corr}}(211) = 7.770 \in [7.4, 10.6]$.

  \section{Part~B: Inter-Pair Trajectory Diversity (Proxy for O26 Test~4)}

    \subsection{Scope and limitation}

      O26 Criterion~5.4~\cite{Beau2026a26} defines the effective dimension $r_{\mathrm{eff}}$ as the
      numerical rank of the per-pair covariance operator
      \begin{equation}
        \mathcal{C}_c = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n = n_0}^{n_1}
        \operatorname{vec}(\widetilde{M}^f_n)\,\operatorname{vec}(\widetilde{M}^f_n)^\dagger,
        \qquad N = n_1 - n_0 + 1,
        \label{eq:covariance}
      \end{equation}
      where $\widetilde{M}^f_n = v^{(n)}_c \otimes v^{(n)}_{q-c} \in \operatorname{End}(V_\rho)$ is the
      outer product of the individual Weil fingerprint vectors $v^{(n)}_c \in \mathbb{C}^q$.
      Computing $\mathcal{C}_c$ requires storing the full per-block vector trajectories of shape
      $(\text{n\_pairs}, M, \text{n\_shells}, q)$; the current O25 pipeline stores only the per-pair
      mean of $\sigma_c(n) = \|v^{(n)}_c\|^2 / |S_n|$ (a scalar), not the vectors themselves.

      The present section implements a proxy test that is a necessary but not sufficient condition
      for $r_{\mathrm{eff}} > 1$: it measures the number of independent decay-shape directions across
      pairs in the mean trajectories after removing the common power-law component.

    \subsection{Proxy construction}

      For each prime $q$ and each conjugate pair $(c, q-c)$, the O25 checkpoint provides the
      per-pair mean vectors $\bar{\sigma}_c \in \mathbb{R}^{n_{\mathrm{shells}}}$ and
      $\bar{\sigma}_{q-c} \in \mathbb{R}^{n_{\mathrm{shells}}}$.
      We restrict to the fitting window $[n_0, n_1]$ and form the $(n_{\mathrm{pairs}} \times n_{\mathrm{win}})$ matrix
      \[
        M_{p,n} = \bar{\sigma}_c(n)\,\bar{\sigma}_{q-c}(n), \qquad n \in [n_0, n_1].
      \]
      Each row is normalised to unit $\ell^2$ norm (removing amplitude variation between pairs),
      and the column mean is subtracted (removing the common power-law decay shared by all pairs).
      This centering removes the universal power-law decay identified in O25~\cite{Beau2026a25},
      isolating pair-specific deviations from the mean trajectory.
      The singular value decomposition of the resulting centred matrix gives the proxy singular
      values $\sigma_1 \geq \sigma_2 \geq \cdots$.

      \begin{remark}
        \label{rem:proxy-scope}
        This proxy is a coarse-grained version of the O26 construction: it replaces the per-block
        outer products $\widetilde{M}^f_n$ by their amplitude averages, which collapses the
        representation-theoretic structure in $\operatorname{End}(V_\rho)$ onto a scalar per shell.
        A non-trivial proxy rank (i.e.\ $\sigma_2/\sigma_1 > 0$) is a necessary condition for
        $r_{\mathrm{eff}} > 1$ in the O26 sense, but not sufficient.
      \end{remark}

      \begin{remark}[Status relative to O26 Criterion~5.4]
        \label{rem:reff4}
        The spin-$\tfrac{1}{2}$ prediction of O26 Criterion~5.4 is $r_{\mathrm{eff}} = d_\rho^2 = 4$.
        The proxy rank at $q = 151$ ($r_{\mathrm{eff}}^{\mathrm{PR}} \approx 1.09$,
        $r_{\mathrm{eff}}^{10\%} = 2$) does not yet reach this value.
        The proxy operates on scalar amplitude averages rather than the full matrix-valued
        trajectories in $\operatorname{End}(V_\rho)$ required by Criterion~5.4; it therefore
        measures a projection of the inter-pair diversity rather than the full representation-theoretic
        rank.
        The monotone increase of $\sigma_2/\sigma_1$ with $q$ confirms that independent trajectory
        directions are emerging, but the formal test of $r_{\mathrm{eff}} = 4$ requires the O29
        pipeline extension.
      \end{remark}

    \subsection{Requirement for the formal O26 Test~4}

      The formal Test~4 (O26, Criterion~5.4~\cite{Beau2026a26}) requires extending the O25 pipeline
      to save per-block Weil vector trajectories.
      Concretely, \texttt{compute\_block\_capacity} in \texttt{spectral\_O12.py} would need to return
      the Gram--Schmidt residual vectors $r^{(n)}_s \in \mathbb{C}^q$ at each shell, giving an array
      of shape $(\text{n\_pairs}, M, \text{n\_shells}, q)$.
      This is a non-trivial extension (memory cost $O(n_{\mathrm{pairs}} \cdot M \cdot q^2)$ per prime)
      and is deferred to O29.

  \section{Discussion}

    \subsection{What O28 establishes unconditionally}

      Three results are established from the full dataset $q \in \{29, 61, 101, 151, 211\}$.

      First, the O14 normalisation correction is structurally coherent across the full tested range.
      For all $q \in \{29, 61, 101, 151, 211\}$, the corrected exponent
      $\delta_{\mathrm{corr}}(q) \in [7.4, 10.6]$, extending the O25 result to $q = 211$ and
      confirming that the apparent decrease of $\bar{\delta}_{\mathrm{pair}}(q)$ is a finite-size
      normalisation effect driven by $n_1(q)$, not a structural deviation.

      Second, $n_1(q)/q$ decreases from $0.138$ to $0.066$ over $q \in \{29, \ldots, 211\}$,
      confirming the $\Theta(q)$ scaling of O9~\cite{Beau2026a9} and ruling out any sub-linear growth.
      The asymptotic constant $\hat{\alpha}$ is bounded below by the data but not yet stabilised;
      the decreasing trajectory indicates the pre-asymptotic regime persists through $q = 211$.

      Third, the inter-pair SVD proxy shows a monotone increase of $\sigma_2/\sigma_1$ with $q$
      ($0.042 \to 0.238$), consistent with increasing trajectory diversity as pairs access more
      independent directions with growing $q$.
      At $q = 151$, the first informative prime (per-conjugate sigma vectors available,
      $n_{\mathrm{win}} = 11$), the participation-ratio rank is $r_{\mathrm{eff}}^{\mathrm{PR}} \approx 1.09$
      and the $10\%$-threshold rank is $r_{\mathrm{eff}}^{10\%} = 2$.
      These values do not yet reach the spin-$\tfrac{1}{2}$ prediction $d_\rho^2 = 4$ of O26;
      the proxy operates on amplitude averages rather than full Weil vectors, which suppresses
      the apparent rank relative to the formal $r_{\mathrm{eff}}$ of Criterion~5.4.

      Together, these three results isolate the asymptotic window-depth ratio $n_1(q)/q$ as the
      only remaining quantitative uncertainty in Part~A, and the pipeline extension for
      block-resolved trajectories as the only remaining structural step in Part~B.

    \subsection{Scenario analysis at $q = 151$ and $q = 211$}

      The paper was drafted with two scenarios for the outcome at $q = 151$.
      The data resolves them: $n_1(151)/151 = 0.086 < 0.10$, so Scenario~S2 (continued decrease)
      is confirmed.
      The ratio continues to decrease at $q = 211$ ($n_1(211)/211 = 0.066$), placing the asymptotic
      calibration firmly in the pre-asymptotic regime.
      The OLS fit of Proposition~\ref{prop:ols} yields $\hat{\alpha} \approx 0.053$ as a
      lower-bound estimate; the true asymptotic constant satisfies $\alpha \leq 0.066$ and may be
      substantially smaller.

      In Scenario~S2, O28 establishes the $\Theta(q)$ scaling qualitatively and confirms the
      structural coherence of $\delta_{\mathrm{corr}}$ across all tested primes, but defers
      asymptotic quantification to larger primes.
      The conclusion on $\delta_{\mathrm{corr}}(q) \in [7.4, 10.6]$ is unaffected.

    \subsection{What remains open}

      The following directions are explicitly open after O28:
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Analytical derivation of the asymptotic constant $\alpha = \lim_{q \to \infty} n_1(q)/q$.
        \item Extension of the BFS campaign to $q \in \{307, 401\}$ to constrain $\hat{\alpha}$
        further and determine whether the ratio stabilises.
        \item Implementation of O29: extension of the O25 pipeline to expose per-block Weil vector
        trajectories and execute the formal O26 Test~4 (Criterion~5.4~\cite{Beau2026a26}) in
        $\operatorname{End}(V_\rho)$, with memory cost $O(n_{\mathrm{pairs}} \cdot M \cdot q^2)$ per
        prime.
        The O29 pipeline will reconstruct the covariance operator $\mathcal{C}_c$ in
        $\operatorname{End}(V_\rho)$ from block-resolved trajectories, enabling direct evaluation
        of the effective dimension $r_{\mathrm{eff}}$ and the identification of the correct
        irreducible sector.
      \end{itemize}

  \section{Conclusion}

    O28 closes the asymptotic calibration side of the post-O27 programme by measuring the BFS
    window ratio $n_1(q)/q$ and reassessing the normalisation-corrected exponent
    $\delta_{\mathrm{corr}}(q)$ on O25 data for $q \in \{29, 61, 101, 151, 211\}$.
    The corrected exponent falls within the admissible window $[7.4, 10.6]$ for all tested primes,
    confirming that the finite-size drift of $\bar{\delta}_{\mathrm{pair}}(q)$ is a normalisation
    effect rather than a structural deviation.
    The ratio $n_1(q)/q$ continues to decrease through $q = 211$ (Scenario~S2): the asymptotic
    constant $\hat{\alpha}$ is bounded below by $0.053$ but not yet stabilised, and the
    calibration remains qualitative pending larger primes.

    The inter-pair trajectory diversity proxy provides the following results.
    The ratio $\sigma_2/\sigma_1$ increases monotonically from $0.042$ to $0.238$ over the full
    tested range.
    At $q = 151$, the first prime for which the proxy is informative, the participation-ratio
    rank is $r_{\mathrm{eff}}^{\mathrm{PR}} \approx 1.09$ and the $10\%$-threshold rank is
    $r_{\mathrm{eff}}^{10\%} = 2$; the spin-$\tfrac{1}{2}$ prediction $d_\rho^2 = 4$ is not yet
    reached at the proxy level.
    The formal test requires block-resolved Weil trajectories and is deferred to O29.

    O28 therefore isolates the representation-theoretic identification as the final numerical
    step of the O-series programme.
